Projects

  • Converting heat energy from railway tracks to electricity
    •

    During my academic journey, I worked on an exciting team project with my classmate. Our project focused on harnessing heat energy from railway tracks and turning it into electricity using Thermo Electric Generators (TEG). These TEG modules work on the principle of Seebeck effect, which produces electric output when there is a temperature difference between the two sides of the module. We connected these modules in series and ensured good heat conductivity with a thermal paste. TEG modules generated electrical power based on the temperature difference between the railway track and the environment, and we stored this power in batteries placed at regular intervals.

    Our efforts paid off when our project won the 2nd prize under seniors category at the 27th National Children's Science Congress (NCSC) in the UAE, organized by the Science India Forum (SIF), U.A.E. This success also led us to showcase our project in the final round of the 27th NCSC in Thiruvananthapuram in, India.
